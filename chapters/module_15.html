Processes & Techniques

The Incident Response Lifecycle

5 Key Phases

A comprehensive approach to managing cybersecurity incidents involves a five-phase lifecycle:

  • 1. Preparation: Establishing an incident response team, defining roles, and developing comprehensive incident response plans and tools.
  • 2. Detection and Analysis: Monitoring systems and networks for signs of incidents, and analyzing data to identify and assess the nature and scope of the threat.
  • 3. Containment and Eradication: Isolating affected systems to prevent further damage, removing the malicious code or unauthorized access, and restoring systems to a secure state.
  • 4. Recovery: Restoring normal operations and services, which may involve restoring data from backups, applying patches, and implementing additional security controls.
  • 5. Post-Incident Analysis: Reviewing the response to identify lessons learned and implement improvements to prevent similar incidents in the future.

The E-discovery Process

A 7-Step Overview

The process of handling electronic evidence for legal cases typically follows these steps:

  • Identification: Identifying all potential sources of relevant electronic evidence (emails, documents, databases, etc.).
  • Preservation: Taking steps (like a legal hold) to ensure the identified data is not altered, deleted, or lost.
  • Collection: Gathering the data from various sources using forensically sound methods and tools.
  • Processing: Organizing, indexing, and filtering the collected data to prepare it for analysis (e.g., de-duplicating files).
  • Review: Legal teams analyze the processed data to identify relevant information for the case.
  • Analysis: Deeper analysis of the reviewed evidence to extract insights, patterns, and trends.
  • Presentation: Presenting the electronic evidence in court through witness testimony, exhibits, or expert reports.

Writing an Incident Report

Essential Components

A detailed incident report is crucial for communication, learning, and decision-making. Key sections include:

  • Executive Summary: A high-level overview of the incident, its impact, and key findings.
  • Incident Background: A detailed description of the incident, including how it was detected, affected systems, and indicators of compromise (IOCs).
  • Incident Response Timeline: A chronological timeline of all events and actions taken from detection to resolution.
  • Recommendations: Actionable recommendations for preventing similar incidents in the future.
  • Appendices: Supplementary materials like logs, forensic reports, and other supporting evidence.
